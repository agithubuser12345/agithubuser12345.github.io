Getting Into Orbit

Getting a rocket into orbit is one of the other complexities of rocket science. Many calculations and input from different fields (i.e., physics, math). Usually rockets are programmed to get into orbit as organizations like NASA have a lot of data and experience to help them do so. Rockets will thrust upward and at a certain velocity, T+(Seconds), and altitude, they will start turning using a method called gimbaled thrust. Gimbaled thrust is created when engines tilt at an angle creating more torque on the rocket. This also changes the rockets orientation turning it to a side. As Earth is rotating at a speed of around 1600 km/h, rockets will usually be launched near the equator for a speed boost. This conserves a lot of fuel during the first couple stages.
